You Are Allowed to Take Up Space

A short, fierce love letter for the woman who has spent too long shrinking. This is your permission slip.

Show notes

  • A short, fierce love letter for the woman who shrinks.
  • Where we first learn to make ourselves small.
  • A body-based practice for taking up space right now.
  • Your permission slip, said plainly.

Transcript

[Cold open]

This is going to be a short one, and I need you to actually listen, not clean while you half-hear it. Put something down. This is your permission slip, and it's overdue.

[Theme music in, under]

Hey love, welcome back to iLuvBnMe. I'm Stefy. No astrology chart today, no elaborate spread. Just a fierce little love letter for the woman who has spent way too long making herself small. If that's you, this whole episode is a hand on your shoulder.

Segment one — where we learned to shrink

Nobody is born apologizing for existing. You learned it. Maybe you were the easy child, the one who didn't cause problems, and you got praised for needing less. Maybe you loved someone whose mood was a weather system, and you got tiny to stay safe. Maybe the world just kept telling you, in a hundred small ways, that a woman is most acceptable when she takes up the least room. None of that was your fault. But un-learning it? That part is yours.

You were not born to be manageable. You were born to be here, fully.

Segment two — take up space in your body first

Here's the thing: taking up space isn't only about speaking up in meetings, though yes, do that. It starts in your body. So right now, wherever you are, if it's safe, I want you to do this with me. Uncross your arms. Uncross your legs. Roll your shoulders back and down. Take up the whole chair. Take one big breath that fills your entire torso, and let your belly actually move. Feel that? That's more of you, present, in the room. That's the whole practice. You just did it.

Segment three — your permission slip

So let me say the thing plainly, so you can borrow my voice until you find your own. You are allowed to take up space. You're allowed to have needs, and to name them. You're allowed to be too much for people who wanted less. You're allowed to be loud in your joy, big in your dreams, and unmissable in a room. You do not have to shrink so that someone else can feel tall.

Before you go

That's it. That's the episode. Short and true. If you needed it, send it to another woman who's been playing small, you both deserve to hear it. The free Self-Love Starter Kit is at the link, and Frequency Shift is where we practice this out loud, together.

[Outro music]

Until next time, take up your space, beautiful. The world adjusted around your smallness, and it will adjust around your fullness too. I love you. Bye for now.
