New Moon Intentions & the Star Card

Under a dark sky, we plant seeds. The Star card lights the way toward hope, healing, and quiet renewal.

Show notes

  • How to set new-moon intentions that actually stick.
  • The Star card as your guide to hope after the fall.
  • A guided intention-setting ritual borrowing the Star's energy.
  • This week's card: the Star, and what she really means.

Transcript

[Cold open]

The sky is dark tonight. No moon at all. And I know a dark sky can feel like an ending. But in the rhythm of the moon, this is the exact moment we plant. Let's talk about beginning again.

[Theme music in, under]

Hey love, welcome back to iLuvBnMe. I'm Stefy, and tonight we are under a new moon, which is my favorite time to dream on purpose. We're going to set intentions that actually stick, with a little help from the most hopeful card in the deck: the Star.

Segment one — what a new moon is for

Where the full moon is about release, the new moon is about planting. The sky goes dark, and in that darkness, we get to begin. It's a natural reset, a monthly invitation to ask: what do I want to grow between now and the next full moon? Not my whole five-year plan. Just this cycle. Small enough to tend, real enough to matter.

Segment two — intentions that actually stick

Here's where most intention-setting goes wrong: we write a wish list and call it a day. "I want more money. I want love. I want peace." Beautiful, but the universe deals in verbs, not wishes. So we're going to phrase our intentions as present-tense, embodied statements. Not "I want to feel calm," but "I am creating a life with more quiet in it." Not "I want love," but "I am becoming someone who receives love easily." Feel the difference? One is begging. The other is deciding.

An intention isn't a wish you send away. It's a promise you make to the woman you're becoming.

Segment three — the ritual, with the Star

So here's our new-moon ritual, and the Star is going to hold our hand through it.

[Music out — just voice]

Light a candle. Picture the Star card if you know it: that woman pouring water under a bright sky, undefended, full of quiet hope. That's the energy we're borrowing. Take three breaths. Now write three intentions for this cycle, present tense, embodied, the way we just practiced. When you've written them, read each one aloud and, like the Star, imagine pouring a little water on it. Then say: "I plant these in trust. I don't have to see the whole path to take the next step." Fold the paper, tuck it somewhere you'll find it at the full moon, and let it grow in the dark.

The card of the week

The Star, obviously, is our card, but I want to leave you with what she really means. She comes right after the Tower, the card of collapse. So if you're setting intentions from the rubble of a hard season, you are in exactly the right place. The Star is proof that hope always gets the page right after the fall.

Before you go

If you set your intentions along with me tonight, I'd love to hear them, and I'd love to keep tending them with you. The free Self-Love Starter Kit is at the link to get you started, and inside Frequency Shift we set and tend our intentions together every single cycle. Share this with the friend who's ready for a fresh start.

[Outro music]

Until next time, plant something in the dark and trust it. That is where every good thing begins. I love you. Bye for now.
