The Empress & the Art of Radical Self-Care

The Empress knows that rest is not a reward. It is your birthright. On slowing down, receiving, and filling your own cup first.

Show notes

  • Who the Empress is, and why she changed the way I rest.
  • The real difference between self-care and self-soothing.
  • A small, uncomfortable practice for learning to receive.
  • This week's card: the Queen of Pentacles.

Transcript

[Cold open]

Quick question before we start. When was the last time you rested without earning it first? Just... rested. No to-do list finished, no permission asked. Hold that thought.

[Theme music in, then under]

Hey love, welcome back to iLuvBnMe. I'm Stefy, and today we are sitting at the feet of my favorite lady in the whole tarot deck: the Empress. She has a lot to teach us about rest, receiving, and the kind of self-care that actually fills you up.

Segment one — meet the Empress

If you've seen her card, you know she is not rushing anywhere. She's reclining in a field of wheat, wrapped in soft fabric, a crown of stars in her hair, everything around her lush and blooming. She is the mother of the deck, the archetype of abundance and creativity. And here's the thing that stopped me in my tracks the first time I really looked at her: she is not producing anything in that image. She is simply receiving. And everything grows anyway.

Segment two — care versus soothing

Let's get honest about self-care, because the word got hijacked. A lot of what we call self-care is actually self-soothing: the glass of wine, the third hour of scrolling, the online cart we fill and never check out. Soothing numbs the feeling. Care tends to the need underneath it. Soothing says "distract me." Care asks "what do you actually need, love?" The Empress is all care. She would never hand you a distraction when what you need is rest.

Rest is not a reward for your productivity. It is the soil everything else grows in.

Segment three — the practice of receiving

So this week I want you to practice the hardest thing for high-functioning, capable women: receiving. Here's your assignment, and it's small. One time this week, when someone offers to help you, say yes. Don't say "oh no, I've got it." Just say "yes, thank you," and let them. Notice how uncomfortable it is. Notice that the world does not end. That discomfort is the exact edge of your growth.

The card of the week

Fittingly, I pulled the Queen of Pentacles for us, the woman who has learned that tending to herself is not separate from tending to her whole abundant life. She keeps a warm home and a full cup at the same time. Let her remind you: you are allowed to be both the giver and the well.

Before you go

If you needed this permission today, it is yours. Grab the free Self-Love Starter Kit at the link, and if you want a whole community practicing this alongside you, come find us inside Frequency Shift. Share this with the friend who's running on empty. Tell her the Empress says lie down.

[Outro music]

Until next time, fill your own cup first. You cannot pour from a pretty, empty thing. I love you. Bye for now.
