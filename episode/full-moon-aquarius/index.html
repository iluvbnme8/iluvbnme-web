Full Moon in Aquarius: Releasing Who You Were

The full moon asks a hard question: who are you still carrying that you were never meant to keep? A reading and a ritual for letting go.

Show notes

  • The astrology of this Aquarius full moon and what it is stirring up in all of us.
  • Why release is not the same thing as forgetting.
  • A guided full-moon releasing ritual you can do tonight with one candle and a scrap of paper.
  • The card of the week: the Eight of Cups, and the brave, quiet exit.

Transcript

[Cold open — quiet, no music]

Who are you still carrying that you were never meant to keep? Sit with that for a second. Don't answer fast. We'll come back to it.

[Theme music swells 6 seconds, then settles underneath]

Hey love, and welcome back to iLuvBnMe, the podcast where we read the cards, the stars, and ourselves a little more gently. I'm Stefy, and if you're listening the night this drops, there is a big, bright, slightly rebellious full moon in Aquarius hanging over us. So grab your tea, get cozy, and let's talk about what she is here to help us release.

Segment one — what this full moon is actually doing

Okay, quick sky report, and I promise to keep it human. A full moon is always a moment of culmination. Whatever seeds you planted at the last new moon, this is where they come up for air. And in Aquarius, the sign of the rebel, the visionary, the one who refuses to belong to the wrong crowd, the theme is liberation. Aquarius doesn't ask "how do I fit in?" She asks "who am I when I stop performing?"

So if the last few weeks have felt itchy, if you've been quietly outgrowing a room you used to love, that is not you being difficult. That is the moon doing her work.

Segment two — release is not forgetting

I want to gently correct something, because I hear this a lot. Releasing someone or something does not mean it didn't matter. It doesn't mean you forget. Release just means you stop asking a chapter that already ended to keep paying your rent. You are allowed to be grateful for who you were at twenty-three and still set her down. She got you here. She doesn't have to drive anymore.

You can honor a season and still let it end.

Segment three — the ritual

So here is what we're going to do tonight. This is my go-to full-moon releasing ritual, and it costs nothing.

[Music drops out for the ritual — just voice]

Find a quiet spot and a small piece of paper. Light a candle. Take three slow breaths with me: in through the nose, and a long sigh out. Now, on that paper, write down the thing you are ready to release. Not what you think you should release. The real one. The name, the habit, the story you keep telling about yourself that isn't true anymore.

When you're ready, read it once, out loud, and then say: "Thank you. I'm setting this down now." And safely, over a sink or a bowl, burn the paper, or if fire isn't your thing, tear it into small pieces. Watch it change form. That is you, changing form too.

The card of the week

I pulled one card for us this week and it's the Eight of Cups, a figure walking away from a row of cups they built, under a moon, toward the mountains. It's the card of the brave, quiet exit. Of leaving something good because you know there's something truer out there. If that's you right now, consider this your permission slip.

Before you go

If this landed, do me a favor and come sit with the rest of us. The free Self-Love Starter Kit is waiting at the link, and if you want to go deeper, Frequency Shift is where we do this work together, month after month. And if you're feeling generous, share this episode with the friend who's mid-release right now. She'll know who she is.

[Outro music swells]

Until next time, be soft with yourself. You are not losing who you were. You are finally becoming who you are. I love you. Bye for now.
