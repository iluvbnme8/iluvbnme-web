When the Cards Say Wait

Divine timing is real, and so is your impatience. An honest talk on trusting the pause and letting things unfold.

Show notes

  • The little family of cards that signal divine timing.
  • Why waiting is an action, not a punishment.
  • An honest test for telling surrender apart from stalling.
  • This week's card: the Star.

Transcript

[Cold open]

You did everything right. You showed up, you did the work, you asked the universe clearly for what you wanted. And still, nothing's moving. Before you decide something is wrong with you, let's talk about what the cards mean when they say: wait.

[Theme music in, under]

Hey love, welcome back to iLuvBnMe. I'm Stefy, and this one is for everyone in the waiting room of their own life right now. I see you. Let's make the wait mean something.

Segment one — the cards of the pause

There's a little family of cards that show up when it's just not time yet. The Hanged Man, suspended upside down, seeing everything from a new angle because he finally stopped struggling. The Seven of Pentacles, a gardener leaning on his tool, looking at fruit that isn't ripe. The Four of Swords, a figure lying down, resting, not defeated. When these show up, the deck isn't saying no. It's saying not yet, and there's a world of difference.

Segment two — waiting is an action

We treat waiting like it's the absence of doing. But divine timing isn't you sitting frozen while the universe runs late. The wait is the work. The Seven of Pentacles gardener isn't idle; he's letting the roots do what roots do underground. You're allowed to rest in the wait. You're allowed to keep tending. You're just released from the exhausting job of forcing a flower to bloom on your schedule.

Not yet is not the same as no. And rushing a ripening only makes it bitter.

Segment three — surrender or stalling?

Now, real talk, because I don't want you to spiritually bypass your own procrastination. There's a difference between surrender and stalling. Here's my honest test: surrender feels like peace. Stalling feels like avoidance dressed up as patience. If waiting brings you a quiet exhale, trust it. If waiting is actually fear wearing a calm mask, that's your nudge to take the one small brave step you've been avoiding. The cards can say wait. They rarely say hide.

The card of the week

I pulled the Star for us, of course I did, because when you're deep in the wait, she's the one who reminds you the water is still flowing, the light is still on, and this pause is a part of the plan, not a detour from it.

Before you go

[Outro music]

Until next time, trust the timing of your life. It has never once been late. I love you. Bye for now.
