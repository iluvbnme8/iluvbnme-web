The Moon Ritual I Return to Again and Again

I have tried the elaborate rituals. The ones with seven crystals arranged by the phase of the moon, the printed correspondences, the incense I could never quite identify. They were beautiful, and I never kept them up. The ritual that actually stayed with me for years is almost embarrassingly simple: a candle, a card, and a single question.

I have tried the elaborate rituals. The ones with seven crystals arranged by the phase of the moon, the printed correspondences, the incense I could never quite identify. They were beautiful, and I never kept them up. The ritual that actually stayed with me for years is almost embarrassingly simple: a candle, a card, and a single question.

I come back to it on new moons and full moons, on ordinary Tuesdays when I feel unmoored, whenever I have drifted a little too far from myself. It takes ten minutes. It asks for almost nothing. And it has quietly rearranged my relationship with my own mind.

The ritual

Here it is, in full. Steal it, change it, make it entirely your own:

  1. Light one candle. Put your phone in another room. Let the small flame be the only urgent thing.
  2. Take three breaths and drop the day. Feel your shoulders come down from your ears. You are off the clock now.
  3. Ask one question. New moon: "What am I planting?" Full moon: "What am I ready to release?" Any night at all: "What do I need to hear?"
  4. Pull a single card. Do not reach for the book. Look at the image and let it answer in its own language.
  5. Write three sentences. Not an essay. Just what the card stirred, and one thing you will carry into tomorrow.
  6. Thank the card. Blow out the candle. That is it. You are done.
A ritual is just love, repeated. It does not have to be elaborate to be holy.

The magic was never in the candle or the card. It is in the ten minutes I give myself, on purpose, to listen. The ritual is a door I keep walking through, and every time, some quieter, truer part of me is waiting on the other side.
