A Love Letter to the Woman You're Growing Into

There is a version of you that already exists on the other side of this season. She is kinder with herself and surer of her yes. And here is the quiet secret: you don't have to reach her to be worthy of your own love right now. You already are her, just earlier in the story.

There is a version of you that already exists on the other side of this season. She is kinder with herself and surer of her yes. And here is the quiet secret: you don't have to reach her to be worthy of your own love right now. You already are her, just earlier in the story.

We are taught to treat ourselves like projects. Fix the flaw, close the gap, arrive at the finished, polished self, and then, finally, rest. But the finished self is a mirage. There is always another horizon. If love waits at the finish line, love waits forever.

The middle is holy

The messy middle, the unsure, in-between, still-figuring-it-out middle, is not a waiting room. It is the whole point. It is where the roots grow. Nothing beautiful has ever bloomed by skipping the part underground, in the dark, growing.

You are not behind. You are growing, and growth has no deadline.

When you pull a card in the middle of it all, notice how rarely the cards scold you. They meet you where you are. The Star does not ask why you aren't further along; she simply pours her water and shows you that hope is a renewable resource. Let that be your posture toward yourself, too.

A small practice

Tonight, before the day folds closed, try this:

  1. Place a hand on your heart and name one way you grew today, however quiet.
  2. Say aloud: "I am allowed to be a work in progress and a masterpiece at once."
  3. Pull a single card and let it be a blessing, not a verdict.

You are allowed to love yourself in the unfinished draft. In fact, that is the only place love has ever lived: in the real, ongoing, gloriously in-progress you.
