On Sovereignty: Choosing Yourself Out Loud

For most of my life I thought choosing myself was something you did quietly, apologetically, with a long explanation attached. I could rest, but only after I had earned it and reassured everyone that I still loved them. I could say no, but only if I softened it into a maybe first. Sovereignty taught me something different. Sovereignty says you are allowed to choose yourself out loud.

Sovereignty is not selfishness

Let us clear this up first, because the fear of being selfish keeps so many good women small. Selfishness takes from others to fill yourself. Sovereignty fills yourself so you have something real to give. One is a taking. The other is a homecoming. When you belong to yourself first, everyone who loves you gets the whole person, not the leftovers.

Sovereignty is the quiet, radical act of belonging to yourself before you belong to anyone else.

Out loud is the practice

The "out loud" part is where it gets brave. It is easy to choose yourself in secret. The growth is in letting people see it. Saying "that does not work for me" without a paragraph of justification. Naming what you want before someone has to guess. Taking your seat at the table instead of hovering near the wall, hoping to be invited.

People may be surprised at first, especially the ones who benefited from your smallness. That surprise is not a sign you have done something wrong. It is the sound of an old agreement being renegotiated in your favor.

A practice in sovereignty

You do not reclaim your sovereignty in one grand gesture. You do it in small, repeated choices:

  1. Say one true no this week, cleanly, with no over-explaining. Let the sentence end where it ends.
  2. Voice one want out loud before anyone asks. "I would love to..." is a complete and worthy sentence.
  3. Notice where you shrink to keep the peace, and stay your full size for ten uncomfortable seconds longer.

You will not get it perfect, and you do not need to. Sovereignty is not a fortress you build once. It is a muscle you use, a door you keep choosing to walk through as your whole, unapologetic self. Choose yourself. And this time, let them hear you do it.
